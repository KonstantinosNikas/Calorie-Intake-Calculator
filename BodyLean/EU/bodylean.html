    About the Calculator

    Purpose:

    Measures the percentage of fat compared to lean tissue (muscle, bone, water, etc.) in your body.

    Use in Calorie Counting:

    Knowing your body fat percentage can help tailor your diet and exercise plan more specifically to your body composition goals. For example, if you're looking to increase muscle mass and decrease body fat, knowing your body fat percentage can help track progress and adjust your calorie intake and macronutrient distribution (proteins, fats, carbohydrates) accordingly.

    Method:

    Body fat can be measured through various methods, such as skinfold measurements, bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA), DEXA scans, and more. These methods vary in accuracy and accessibility.
